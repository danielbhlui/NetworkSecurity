The last assignment of this course requires me to reflect upon critically the events as well as my learnings during this course.

Module Reflection

A critical incident has happened: I got only 50% in my first assignment. So what? It shows that my learning approach is wrong. Now what? I need a different behavior approach and think deeply about what produces my learning.

After critical analysis, I have changed my behavior to be more actively learning style by doing more active researches and reading more. That has produced more learning for me. Throughout the rest of the module, I have learned to identify and analyze various security threats and vulnerabilities in network systems. I have been able to detect vulnerabilities and assess the impacts on the system. I find that this module emphasized the importance of a systematic approach to vulnerability assessment and that this structured methodology ensures that potential threats are identified and addressed effectively. As far as designing and critically appraising solutions is concerned, by critically evaluating existing security measures and identifying areas for improvement, I have been able to mitigate risks and enhance overall security. As far as gathering and synthesizing information is concerned, this module has taught me to utilize various resources, including internet security alerts, warning sites, journal articles, etc.

Secondly, this student does have evidence of autonomous work, as well as evidence of working in collaboration with others. When working independently, this student has produced the first assignment and now the second assignment (the executive summary) and this very reflection piece. When working with others, we have created discussion in collaborative discussions one and two. In collaborative discussion two, this student has made a peer response to Andrius¡¦ post, and he has responded back to me, thanking my ¡§thoughtful comments¡¨ and the additional perspectives that I have provided. Thirdly, working on this module has been a challenging yet enlightening experience. Throughout the course, I have often felt hurried due to the sheer volume of tasks that needed to be completed. The workload appeared to be quite heavy, and there was a constant need to conduct more researches and complete additional work. This sense of urgency and pressure significantly impacted my approach to the module.

The feeling of being hurried and overwhelmed sometimes affected the quality of my work. For example, I only got a passing grade on my first assignment. There were moments when I had to prioritize speed over thoroughness, which occasionally led to less detailed outputs. However, this pressure also pushed me to develop better time management skills and find more efficient ways to handle the workload. Despite the challenges, I remained committed to completing the module to the best of my ability.

Throughout the module, I reviewed the views of my peers and instructors, which provided valuable insights and different perspectives on the topics covered. Engaging with others¡¦ viewpoints helped me refine my understanding and approach to penetration testing.

Yes, I have thought about what exactly produced my learning during this module. Under ordinary circumstance, I agree that studying on one hand and using/practicing the subject matters being studied on the other hand would make a powerful combination that can let me understand more and retain more on the subject matters. However, during these six weeks, I am afraid the heavy work load has taken a toll on me. All I can do is to hurry up and meet the deadlines. I just could not afford to slow down a little to smell the roses along the pent test journey.

And yes, I do have evidence that I have acquired a set of skills and knowledge that are crucial for identifying and mitigating security vulnerabilities. I have become proficient in using tools like Nmap to perform network scanning and enumeration. This involves identifying active devices, open ports, and services running on a network. Moreover, this module has provided hands-on experience with vulnerability assessment tools such as Nessus and ZAP. I have learned to identify, classify, and prioritize vulnerabilities based on their severity. In a controlled environment, I have tried out the exploitation of a vulnerable service in the Altoro Mutual. This exercise highlighted the importance of having fortified systems to prevent exploitation by malicious hackers.

This module has also taught me about anomalies. I have learned to identify common web vulnerabilities such as SQL injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and cross-site request forgery (CSRF). This student finds it very interesting to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities. This also helps me to understand how to secure website and protect against potential attacks in future.

Last but not least, the module has also taught me the ethical, social and legal aspects of hacking. I once again have been reminded to follow GDPR in analyzing the legal side of things.

In conclusion, the skills and knowledge developed through this networking course have been instrumental in my ability to conduct thorough and effective security assessments. By applying these skills in future, I will help others identify and mitigate security risks, thereby enhancing their security posture.
